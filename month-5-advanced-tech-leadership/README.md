# Month 5 — Advanced Tech Leadership

> **Track:** Tech Lead
> **Duration:** 4 hours
> **Focus:** Technology vision, innovation culture, strategic technical thinking
> **Difficulty:** Advanced

---

## Session Overview

This session develops the leadership capabilities that set technical direction:
crafting a compelling technology vision, building a culture where innovation
thrives, and codifying how a team works through a living charter.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

- Develop and articulate a technology vision
- Build and sustain innovation culture in teams
- Create effective team charters
- Balance innovation with delivery
- Make strategic technology investments

---

## Core Concepts

### Innovation Culture Elements

| Element | What It Provides |
|----------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Psychological Safety | Safe to experiment and fail |
| Time for Exploration | Dedicated innovation time |
| Resource Access | Tools and budget for experimentation |
| Recognition | Celebrating attempts, not just successes |
| Leadership Support | Visible executive backing |

### Team Charter Elements

- Mission and purpose
- Team values and norms
- Decision-making process
- Communication protocols
- Success metrics
- Individual growth areas

### 2. Innovation Framework (25 points)

# Innovation Framework: Platform Team

## Innovation Philosophy

### Why Innovation Matters
- Technology landscape changes rapidly
- Competitors are innovating
- Team engagement and retention
- Business growth opportunities

### Our Innovation Approach
Balance between:
- 70% Delivery (current commitments)
- 20% Improvement (making existing better)
- 10% Exploration (new possibilities)

---

## Innovation Programs

### Program 1: Innovation Time (10%)

**Structure:**
- Every engineer gets 4 hours/week for exploration
- Can be used flexibly (weekly or batched)
- No approval needed for topic

**Expectations:**
- Document learnings
- Share with team monthly
- Not tied to performance evaluation

**Guardrails:**
- Must not impact committed work
- Should relate to team's domain
- Not just "more of the same work"

### Program 2: Hack Days (Quarterly)

**Structure:**
- 2-day quarterly event
- Cross-functional teams welcome
- Presentations at end

**Themes (Rotating):**
- Q1: Developer Experience
- Q2: Customer Impact
- Q3: Technical Excellence
- Q4: Wild Card

**Support Provided:**
- $500/team for tools/resources
- Executive sponsors attend demos
- Top projects get follow-up investment

### Program 3: Innovation Proposals

**Structure:**
- Anyone can propose larger innovations
- Structured proposal format
- Monthly review by leadership

**Criteria for Approval:**
1. Aligned with team vision
2. Clear hypothesis and success metrics
3. Reasonable time investment (max 1 engineer-month)
4. Path to production or clear learnings

---

## Creating Psychological Safety

### What We Commit To

**As Leaders:**
- Celebrate thoughtful failures
- Never punish good-faith experiments
- Share our own failures openly
- Ask "what did we learn?" not "who failed?"

**As a Team:**
- Support each other's experiments
- Give constructive feedback
- Share failures as eagerly as successes
- Assume good intent

### Practices

**Failure Retrospectives:**
- Regular sessions to discuss failed experiments
- Focus on learnings, not blame
- Document and share widely

**Experimentation Log:**
- Public log of all experiments
- Includes failures and learnings
- Referenced in planning

## How We Work

**Types of Decisions:**

| Type | Who Decides | Process |
|------------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Architectural | Tech Lead + team | RFC process |
