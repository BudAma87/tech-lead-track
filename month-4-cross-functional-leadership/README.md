# Month 4 — Cross-Functional Leadership & Influence

> **Track:** Tech Lead
> **Duration:** 4 hours
> **Focus:** Stakeholder management, influence without authority, conflict resolution, partnerships
> **Difficulty:** Advanced

---

## Session Overview

This session develops the ability to lead across organizational boundaries —
where you have responsibility but not authority. The running scenario is a
**real-time analytics platform** whose success depends on Product, Design,
Operations, Data, and Finance, each with competing priorities and, in one case, a
history of conflict. The Tech Lead must map stakeholders, influence them
ethically, resolve conflict, and formalize a working partnership.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

- Map stakeholders by power, interest, and stance
- Build influence strategies grounded in others' motivations
- Repair damaged relationships and build coalitions
- Resolve common cross-functional conflicts with clear frameworks
- Formalize cross-team collaboration through partnership agreements

## Core Concepts

### Leading Without Authority

| Lever | How It Works |
|--------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Reciprocity | Create value first, then make the ask |
| Coalition | Enlist allies who carry influence |
| Framing | Position the ask in the other party's interests |
| Trust repair | Acknowledge, change behavior, follow through |

### Cross-Functional Dynamics in This Scenario

| Function | Current Stance |
|-----------|-----------------------------------------|
| Product | Supportive, different priority view |
| Design | Skeptical, overloaded team |
| Operations | Neutral, doesn't see it as priority |
| Data | Resistant, past conflict with Engineering |
| Finance | Questioning ROI / budget |

## Facilitator Notes

### Common Pitfalls

- Relying on authority you don't have
- Making the ask before creating value
- Avoiding the past conflict instead of naming it
- Vague partnership agreements with no metrics or escalation path
- Treating Finance's budget question as an obstacle rather than a fair ask

### Discussion Prompts

- "How do you influence someone who outranks you?"
- "How do you rebuild trust after a public blame incident?"
- "When is escalation the right move vs. a failure to lead?"

# Challenge: Real-Time Analytics Platform

> **Time Allocation:** 3 hours (during session)
> **Difficulty:** Advanced

You are leading a strategic initiative that requires coordination across
Engineering, Product, Design, and Operations. Navigate complex stakeholder
dynamics and build effective partnerships.

## Scenario Context

### Initiative: Real-Time Analytics Platform

You're the Tech Lead, but success requires:
- Product team to prioritize features
- Design team for dashboard UX
- Operations for infrastructure support
- Data team for pipeline integration
- Finance for budget approval

### Current Challenges

- Product has a different priority view than Engineering
- Design team is overloaded with other projects
- Operations doesn't see this as their priority
- Data team and Engineering have past conflicts
- Finance is questioning the budget

### 1. Stakeholder Mapping (25 points)

**File:** `{your-name}-month4-stakeholders.md`

power/interest grid, a relationship map, and an engagement plan.

**Evaluation Criteria**
- Stakeholder identification (5 pts)
- Analysis depth (5 pts)
- Mapping accuracy (5 pts)

### 2. Influence Strategy Document (25 points)

**File:** `{your-name}-month4-influence.md`

Required: influence objectives, per-target strategies (understanding, tactic,
steps, script), and an influence timeline.

**Evaluation Criteria**
- Objective clarity (5 pts)
- Stakeholder understanding (5 pts)
- Tactic appropriateness (5 pts)
- Script quality (5 pts)
- Timeline realism (5 pts)

### 3. Conflict Resolution Playbook (25 points)
