# Influence Strategy: Real-Time Analytics Platform

> **Author:** Buddhika Amarasinghe · **Role:** Tech Lead

## Influence Objectives

### Objective 1: Secure Design Resources
**Target:** Mike (Design Director)
**Current State:** Skeptical, team overloaded
**Desired State:** Commits 1 designer for 3 months

### Objective 2: Repair Data Team Relationship
**Target:** Jennifer (Data Lead)
**Current State:** Resistant due to past conflict
**Desired State:** Active collaboration partner

### Objective 3: Get Budget Approved
**Target:** David (Finance Director)
**Current State:** Questioning ROI
**Desired State:** Budget approved with contingency

---

## Influence Strategies

### Strategy 1: Design Resources

#### Understanding Their World
**Mike's Pressures:**
- 3 other high-priority projects
- Team burnout concerns
- Last project went over timeline

**What Mike Values:**
- Predictable timelines
- Clear scope
- Team wellbeing
- Professional growth opportunities

#### Influence Approach
**Primary Tactic:** Reciprocity + Reduced Risk

**Step 1: Create Value First**
- Offer to help with their current UX tech debt
- Provide engineering support for their tooling needs
- Timeline: Next 2 weeks

**Step 2: Frame the Ask**
- Position as growth opportunity for their team
- Emphasize contained scope (clear timeline)
- Highlight visibility with executives

**Step 3: Reduce Perceived Risk**
- Propose phased commitment (1 month trial)
- Offer to backfill if their priorities shift
- Create clear exit criteria

**Script:**
> "Mike, I know your team is stretched. Before I ask for anything, I wanted to
> offer some help — we have bandwidth to address that animation library issue your
> team mentioned. And for this project, I'm thinking about a phased approach where
> we start with just 4 weeks of partnership. If it's not working for your team, no
> hard feelings. But I think this could be great visibility for [designer] who
> mentioned wanting more strategic work."

---

### Strategy 2: Data Team Relationship Repair

#### Understanding the Conflict History
**What Happened:**
- Previous project: Engineering blamed Data for delays
- No proper post-mortem or acknowledgment
- Jennifer felt thrown under the bus publicly

#### Influence Approach
**Primary Tactic:** Relationship Repair + Coalition

**Step 1: Acknowledge and Apologize**
- 1:1 meeting, take responsibility
- Don't make excuses or justify
- Ask what would help going forward

**Step 2: Demonstrate Changed Behavior**
- Include Data team in early architecture discussions
- Share credit proactively
- Buffer timelines for integration

**Step 3: Build Coalition**
- Get Sarah (VP Product) to vouch for the approach
- Have Data team's manager see the collaboration

**Script:**
> "Jennifer, I wanted to talk about the analytics project, but first I owe you an
> apology for how the recommendation engine project went. We didn't communicate
> well, and when things went sideways, you got unfairly blamed. That wasn't right,
> and I should have spoken up. I'd like to do this project differently — starting
> with understanding what you need to feel good about the collaboration."

---

### Strategy 3: Budget Approval

#### Understanding Their World
**David's Pressures:**
- Accountable for spend discipline against company targets
- Wary of open-ended run costs on new platforms
- Needs a defensible paper trail for approvals

**What David Values:**
- Clear, quantified ROI
- Staged/predictable spend
- Contingency and cost controls
- Alignment with revenue goals leadership already committed to

#### Influence Approach
**Primary Tactic:** Evidence + Framing + Staged Commitment

**Step 1: Speak His Language**
- Build a one-page business case: cost, expected return, payback period
- Tie the return to Sarah's Q3 revenue/differentiation targets (coalition)

**Step 2: De-risk the Ask**
- Propose phased budget with approval gates (don't ask for it all at once)
- Include a contingency line and a "kill/continue" checkpoint after MVP

**Step 3: Make the Decision Easy**
- Offer 2–3 clearly costed options with a recommendation
- Pre-align with VP Eng so Finance sees engineering leadership backs the number

**Script:**
> "David, I know you're rightly questioning the spend. I've put together a one-page
> case that ties this to the Q3 revenue targets Product is committed to. Rather than
> asking for the full budget up front, I'm proposing we release it in two stages
> with a go/no-go checkpoint after the MVP — so we prove the ROI before the larger
> spend. There's a 15% contingency built in. Can I walk you through it Thursday?"
