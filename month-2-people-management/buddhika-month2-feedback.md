# Feedback Practice

> **Author:** Buddhika Amarasinghe · **Role:** Tech Lead
> **Framework:** SBI + Request (Situation → Behavior → Impact → Request)

## Framework: SBI + Request

### Feedback 1: Positive — Jordan's Improvement

**Situation:**
"In yesterday's code review for the payment service..."

**Behavior:**
"...you identified a critical security flaw in the input validation that everyone
else had missed."

**Impact:**
"This prevented a potential vulnerability from reaching production and showed real
growth in your attention to security concerns."

**Request:**
"I'd love for you to share this approach in our next team meeting — it would help
others learn."

---

### Feedback 2: Constructive — Sam's Comfort Zone

**Situation:**
"Over the past quarter, I've noticed in our project assignments..."

**Behavior:**
"...you've consistently taken on familiar tasks rather than volunteering for new
challenges like the Kubernetes migration."

**Impact:**
"While your work remains high quality, this pattern limits your growth and means
others don't benefit from your experience in new areas."

**Request:**
"For next quarter, I'd like you to lead at least one project outside your comfort
zone. Can we discuss which one interests you?"

---

### Feedback 3: Difficult — Riley's Performance

**Situation:**
"Over the past month, specifically in the checkout service project..."

**Behavior:**
"...you've missed two sprint commitments and the code that was delivered had three
P2 bugs."

**Impact:**
"The team had to scramble to cover the gaps, and our stakeholders are concerned
about timeline reliability."

**Request:**
"I want to support you — can we discuss what's going on and create a plan to get
back on track?"

---

### Feedback 4: Peer Conflict — Morgan and Alex

**Situation:**
"In last week's architecture review meeting..."

**Behavior:**
"...there was visible tension when your proposal was challenged. I noticed raised
voices and interrupting."

**Impact:**
"Other team members seemed uncomfortable and the technical discussion didn't reach
a clear conclusion."

**Request:**
"How can we ensure these discussions stay productive? What would help you engage
more constructively?"

---

### Feedback 5: Growth Opportunity — Casey

**Situation:**
"In your work on the notification service this sprint..."

**Behavior:**
"...you asked great questions and paired effectively with Morgan, but you didn't
write any code independently."

**Impact:**
"While the pairing is valuable, you need independent practice to build confidence
and show readiness for more responsibility."

**Request:**
"For next sprint, I'd like you to take on one small feature independently. We can
identify a good candidate together."

---

### Feedback 6: Recognition — Morgan

**Situation:**
"Throughout this quarter, especially during our challenging release cycles..."

**Behavior:**
"...you've consistently supported teammates, volunteered for unglamorous tasks,
and maintained a positive attitude even under pressure."

**Impact:**
"Your contributions make the team stronger and create an environment where others
feel supported. You exemplify our collaboration values."

**Request:**
"I'd like to recognize this in our team meeting and would also like you to consider
how to share these collaborative practices more formally."
