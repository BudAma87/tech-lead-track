# Month 3 — Project Delivery & Execution

> **Track:** Tech Lead
> **Duration:** 4 hours
> **Focus:** Risk management, delivery planning, capacity, stakeholder comms
> **Difficulty:** Advanced

---

## Session Overview

This session develops the delivery-leadership skills a Tech Lead needs to drive a
complex, high-stakes project to completion: identifying and mitigating risk,
building a realistic timeline with a defensible critical path, planning capacity
honestly against constraints, and keeping stakeholders informed and aligned. The
running scenario is a **monolith-to-microservices migration** that must protect
business operations while it happens.

### Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, participants will be able to:

- Build a rigorous project risk register with mitigation and monitoring
- Create a phased delivery timeline with milestones, dependencies, and buffers
- Identify the critical path and manage float
- Plan team capacity realistically and close capacity gaps
- Design a stakeholder communication and escalation plan

---

## Core Concepts

### Delivery Leadership Dimensions

| Dimension | Question to Answer |
|-----------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Risk | What could go wrong, and what do we do about it? |
| Timeline | What, by when, in what order, with what slack? |
| Capacity | Do we have the people to do it — honestly? |
| Communication | Who needs to know what, how often, and why? |

### Delivery Artifacts

- Risk register (probability × impact, mitigation, monitoring)
- Phased timeline with milestones and exit criteria
- Critical path and buffer strategy
- Capacity plan (velocity, gaps, mitigation options)
- Stakeholder map + communication matrix + escalation framework

## Facilitator Notes

### Common Pitfalls

- Optimistic timelines with no buffer
- Risk registers that are written once and never monitored
- Ignoring part-time availability in capacity math
- Communicating only when things go wrong
- Confusing the longest phase list with the true critical path

### Discussion Prompts

- "How much buffer is honest vs. padding?"
- "When do you escalate, and to whom?"
- "How do you keep 99.9% uptime during a migration?"

# Challenge: Monolith-to-Microservices Migration

> **Time Allocation:** 3 hours (during session)
> **Difficulty:** Advanced

You are leading a critical project: migrating from a monolithic application to
microservices while maintaining business operations. Create comprehensive project
delivery materials.

## Project Context

### Project: Monolith to Microservices Migration

**Scope:**
- Decompose monolith into 8 microservices
- Maintain 99.9% uptime during migration
- Complete in 9 months
- Team: 6 engineers

**Key Constraints:**
- Black Friday freeze (November)
- Two team members half-time (other project)
- Limited testing environment capacity
